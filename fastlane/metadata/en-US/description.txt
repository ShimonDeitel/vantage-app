Every serious drawing course teaches the same sighting tools: a pencil held at arm's length and a plumb line to check verticals. They work, but they take years to internalize, and there is no feedback loop. Until a teacher points at your page, you cannot tell how far off your proportions actually are.

Vantage turns your iPhone into those classical tools. The Overlay tab shows your camera feed with a proportion grid (2 to 6 divisions) and a plumb crosshair that reads the phone's motion sensors to stay perfectly level no matter how you tilt the device, just like a hand-held plumb bob. Hold the phone steady and the lines snap into a crisp locked state with a haptic tick, so you can sight your subject and transfer measurements to paper. In the Critique tab, photograph your reference and your finished sketch. Vantage asks an AI vision model for a structured set of proportion ratios from each image, then computes the actual percentage differences on your device, giving you specific feedback like "the forearm reads about 20 percent shorter than the reference" instead of vague encouragement. The ghost correction overlay then draws the corrected proportion marks in faint red pencil, stroke by stroke, directly on the photo of your own sketch, so the fix is shown in place, not just described.

The live camera overlay is free.
