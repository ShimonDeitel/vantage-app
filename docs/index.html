How It Works

Three steps, in the order you actually draw.

Sight your subject, capture both photos when you're done, then see precisely where the proportions drifted.

01SIGHT

Live grid & plumb line Free

Point the camera at your subject or reference. A proportion grid (2×2 to 6×6) and a plumb crosshair read the phone's own gyroscope and stay world-vertical as you move — the same trick as a hand-held plumb bob, done electronically. Hold still and the lines spring into a locked, brightened state.

02CAPTURE

Photograph reference & sketch Pro

When you're finished (or partway through), photograph your reference and your paper sketch. Vantage sends each photo to a vision model separately and asks for a structured set of proportion ratios — head, shoulders, hips, arms, legs, torso, hands.

03CRITIQUE

Real percentages, not a guess Pro

Vantage computes the actual difference between the two ratio sets itself — "the arm length reads about 20% shorter than the reference" — instead of asking the model to eyeball the arithmetic in prose.

The Quirky Feature

The ghost-limb correction overlay.

Instead of only describing the fix in a sentence, Vantage redraws the reference's correct proportions as a faint red-pencil bracket directly on top of a photo of your own sketch — right where the fix belongs. In the app, each mark draws itself stroke-by-stroke, like a teacher's red pencil, right after your critique comes back.

Scroll this into view once more to watch the demo line redraw itself.
